**Karma and its Consequences:** [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=270)

* Every action (karma) has a consequence or fruit.  No action is fruitless, although the timing of the enjoyment of fruits may vary. [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=325)
* Actions are typically performed with desire (fame, wealth, power, etc.). [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=1160)
* The Gita associates auspicious and inauspicious consequences with karma: good work yields good results, and bad work yields bad results. [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=486)
* Both auspicious and inauspicious actions bind the soul to the cycle of birth and rebirth (samsara). [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=471)

**Types of Karma:**

The lecture outlines three types of karma: [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=543)
[slide 3]

* **Karma:** Actions performed according to prescribed duties. [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=571)
* **Akarma:** Inaction.
* **Vikarma:** Actions against prescribed duties. [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=571)

A further classification based on desire is introduced: [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=721)

* **Karma (with desire):** Action performed with a desire for its fruit, leading to attachment and the cycle of birth and death.  This is the type of action most people engage in.
* **Nishkama Karma (without desire):** Action performed without attachment to its results, driven by a sense of duty. [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=571)

**The Importance of Action:** [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=494)

* The Bhagavad Gita does *not* advocate inaction (akarma). [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=1429)
* It advocates for the renunciation of the *fruits* of karma, not the renunciation of karma itself. [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=1747)
* Even breathing is considered karma; no being can escape karma by refraining from action. [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=1474)
* Renunciation of action is only possible upon achieving salvation or self-realization. [ðŸ”‰](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPNlKpTK1Ko?t=1527)

**Key Takeaway:**

The core message of the lecture is the crucial distinction between renouncing the *fruits* of karma and renouncing *karma* itself.  *Nishkama Karma* is not inaction but rather action performed without attachment to results, driven by a sense of duty and moral commitment.  This is a complex concept requiring further exploration.
